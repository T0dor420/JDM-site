Nissan Skyline

The Skyline name first appeared in 1957 with the Prince Skyline, produced by the Prince Motor Company, which merged with Nissan in 1966. The Skyline evolved from a modest sedan into a high-performance sports car over the years. The Skyline GT-R, especially from the R32 generation onward, is celebrated for its advanced technology and exceptional performance on both the road and the track. Key features include advanced all-wheel-drive systems (ATTESA E-TS) and the potent RB26DETT engine in earlier models. The Skyline GT-R gained international fame due to its dominance in motorsports, particularly in the Japanese Touring Car Championship (JTCC) and its victory at the Bathurst 1000 in Australia. Its appearances in video games and its role in popular culture, such as in the "Fast and Furious" franchise, have also contributed to its status as an automotive icon. In summary, the Nissan Skyline, particularly the GT-R models, stands as a testament to Nissan's engineering prowess and has left an indelible mark on the automotive world, blending performance, technology, and cultural significance into a single iconic nameplate.
